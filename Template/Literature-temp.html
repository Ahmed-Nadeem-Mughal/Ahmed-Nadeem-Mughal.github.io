The Weight of Signals

Published: March 2025

He stared at the terminal long after the script had finished running. The data was all there — names, coordinates, timestamps — but he could not bring himself to read it. Knowing, he understood, was its own kind of violence.

The coffee had gone cold an hour ago, a forgotten prop in a scene that had already changed. Outside, the city hummed its indifferent neon hum, but in here, time had folded into a single, unbearable weight.

"The truth is not a gift. It is a weapon. And the person who hands it to you rarely checks if you are armed."

He thought of her then. Not the data‑points — the age, the last‑known address, the flagged transactions — but the person behind them. The gap between a life and its digital reflection had never felt wider.

The cursor blinked. Waiting. He pressed Ctrl+C and closed the terminal window.

Some signals, he decided, weren't meant to be followed.
