Oil on Canvas

The Observer Effect

This piece began as a study of surveillance and self‑perception. The central figure — half‑illuminated, half‑shadowed — confronts the viewer with an expression that hovers between defiance and complicity.

I used a limited palette of ultramarine, burnt sienna, and titanium white to create a world that feels simultaneously hyper‑real and dreamlike. The glitched‑out edges of the canvas represent the intrusion of digital noise into organic memory.

Completed over three months in my studio, this work represents a turning point in my practice — the moment I stopped painting what I saw and started painting what I knew.

Title The Observer Effect
Medium Oil on Canvas
Dimensions 90 × 120 cm
Year 2025
